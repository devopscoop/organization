GitHub, GitLab, and Codeberg are three major git-based platforms suitable for hosting decentralized, free, open source projects. Each has a unique philosophy, feature set, and community focus, which may impact your decision depending on project needs for decentralization, privacy, and sustainability.

GitHub

GitHub offers the largest developer community, seamless GitHub Actions CI/CD, and great discoverability, but is highly centralized (controlled by Microsoft), mixing FOSS and proprietary business models. It's ideal for exposure but less philosophically decentralized.

GitLab

GitLab is highly flexible: you can use its cloud or self-host your own instance (with Forgejo or Gitea for extra decentralization). CI/CD is robust, permissions are granular, and it's more open source friendly, though the main hosted service is for-profit.

Codeberg

Codeberg is a nonprofit, FOSS-driven platform based on Gitea, promoting true decentralization via federation and ethical standards. There's no tracking or ads, making it suitable for privacy-conscious projects. The community is smaller and less discoverable than GitHub.

Best Choice for Decentralized FOSS Projects

For those who prioritize decentralization, privacy, no vendor lock-in, and nonprofit hosting, Codeberg is the most philosophically aligned-offering FOSS infrastructure, federated collaboration, no ads, and ethical stewardship. For maximum exposure and world-class CI/CD, GitHub is a strong secondary choice. GitLab provides flexibility if you plan to run your own instance or want integrated DevOps features.

Summary:

  • Most decentralized and ethical: Codeberg

  • Best for discoverability: GitHub

  • Best for custom workflows or self-hosting: GitLab

Each platform supports free open source hosting, but the best fit depends on your project's values and requirements. For a truly decentralized, free, open source project, Codeberg stands out as the leading option.
